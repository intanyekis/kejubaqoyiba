Who is Sandra Diaz-Twine?

Sandra Diaz-Twine is an American television personality.

Sandra Diaz-Twine is the winner of the first two seasons of the game show, Survivor; Survivor: Pearl Islands and Survivor: Heroes vs. Villans making her the show’s only two-time winner.

On May 15, 2019, during the live reunion show of Survivor: Edge of Extinction, it was revealed she and fellow Survivor contestant Rob Mariano would be permanent mentors on Survivor: Island of the Idols. She will be in the upcoming fortieth season of Survivor, Survivor: Winners at War.

She currently works as a law firm secretary.

Sandra Diaz- Twine: Age, Parents, Ethnicity

Sandra Diaz-Twine was born on 30 July 1974. She was born in Stamford, Connecticut, the United States, and raised in Fayetteville, North Carolina. She is 47 years old.

Details about her father and mother are not disclosed. Her ethnicity is unknown.

Before going on Survivor, she worked as a senior office associate in the Army and Air Force Exchange Service. Previous to her employment at Fort Lewis, Washington, she served in the U.S.

Army as a chemical repair specialist. She also previously worked in the mailroom of the Army and Air Force Exchange Service and in customer service at a clothing store.

Education

She graduated from a community college where she received an Associates Degree in Business Administration from Fayetteville Community College in North Carolina.

Career of Sandra Diaz-Twine

After her long Army career for almost 15 years, Sandra Diaz-Twine participated in the adventure game show and reality TV series Survivor in 2003, 7th season; Survivor: Pearl Island.

She won the show and received prize money of around $1 million. She won by a vote of 6–1 with Morris. Sandra gained the votes of Opray, Boneham, Hastie, Roberts, Johnson, and Dalton (other participants).

She again participated in the 12th season of the reality show; Survivor: Heroes vs. Villains. She is considered the ‘Queen of Survivor.’

On May 16, 2010, Diaz-Twine received six jury votes from Yates, Heroes tribe members Thomas, Amanda Kimmel, Candice Woodcock, Boneham, and Colby Donaldson to Shallow’s three and Hantz’s zero to win the game. Moreover, she is the first and only two-times winner of the game show Survivor to date.

Eventually, she also appeared in the 34th season of the TV series Survivor: Game Changers. Unfortunately, she did not win this time.

As of 2019, she is appearing in the 39th cycle of the competitive reality TV series Survivor: Island of the Idols.

Sandra’s Appearance in TV shows

Apart from her appearance in the Gameshow, she appeared in the TV shows including Late Show with David Letterman, Howard Stern, TV Guide Special: Survivor Millionaires, TV Guide Specials: Survivor – Heroes vs Villains Preview, Late Night with Jimmy Fallon, and The Early Show.
