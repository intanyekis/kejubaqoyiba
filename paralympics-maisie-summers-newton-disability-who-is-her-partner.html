Maisie Summers-Newton with her disability sets a new record at Paralympics. Read to learn more about the youngest champion.

Maisie Summers-Newton is another Great Britain Talent, who has won the gold in the 2020 Tokyo Paralympics. She has set the new world record in SM6 200m medley Individual. She has broken the 9-year record of the British swimmer Ellie Simmonds who was the champion of 2012 London and 2016 Rio.

Learn about Paralympian Maisie Summers-Newton Disability?

Her disability is originally congenital with Short Stature. She has been suffering from achondroplasia since birth.

Maisie Summer competes in the S6 disability category.

In 2014 Maisie broke her arm and as a result of which she was unable to compete in the 2016 Championship. But she was able to swim again after the cast was removed.

Maisie watched British Paralympic Swimmer, Simmonds’ performance in 2012 London while she was at the age of 10. Simmonds also suffers from achondroplasia. Maise was inspired by Simmond’s incredible achievements to pursue her career in Swimming.

Six years later in the 2018 European Championship’s SB6 100m backstrokes, she competed against Simmonds and set the world record to win the gold medal. She expresses this to be one of her memorable achievements.

Maise was also announced as the BBC Young personality of the year 2018.

Maisie reportedly admits that she enjoys racing against Simmonds. It seems with the persistent effort she has broken the record of her champion idol. She is next aiming to win the 2022 Commonwealth Games. Furthermore, she is also a part of the athlete advisory group for the 2022 Commonwealth Games.

She is currently a member of the Northampton Swimming Club and is coached by Jacquie Marshall.
