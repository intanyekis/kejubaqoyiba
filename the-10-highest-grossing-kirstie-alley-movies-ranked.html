The movie industry is always buzzing with excitement, as audiences flock to theaters to see the latest blockbuster hits and independent gems. While box office figures are not the only measure of a film's success, they do provide a glimpse into the popularity and cultural impact of a movie. With that in mind, we present to you the top 10 highest-grossing Kirstie Alley movies. From heart-pounding action to heartwarming drama, these films showcase Kirstie Alley's range and talent as an actress as well as her impact on the movie industry before her death in December 2022. So, sit back, relax, and join us as we examine the biggest hits of Kirstie Alley's career.

She’s Having a Baby - 1988 ($35.7 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Released in 1988, She's Having a Baby is a romantic comedy film starring Kirstie Alley as the female lead. Produced by Paramount Pictures, the film follows the story of a young couple as they experience the highs and lows of marriage and parenthood. At the box office, the movie was a success, earning a total of $35.7 million.

Sibling Rivalry - 1990 ($38.7 Million)

Via Flickr/Celebritizer

Kirstie Alley starred as the main character, Carol Owens, in the 1990 comedy film Sibling Rivalry, alongside actors Bill Pullman and Jami Gertz. The movie, produced by TriStar Pictures, tells the story of a woman trying to manage her career and family life. Sibling Rivalry was a smash hit in the movie industry and that is evident as it grossed $38.7 million at the box office.

It Takes Two - 1995 ($41 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Playing the role of Diane Barrows, one of the main characters, Kirstie Allen appeared in the 1995 comedy film It Takes Two, alongside actors Steve Guttenberg and Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen. Produced by Warner Bros., the movie tells the story of two young girls who switch places at summer camp in search of a better life. It Takes Two was a commercial success, grossing $41 million at the box office.

Madhouse - 1990 ($45.6 Million)

Via Flickr/Past Posters

In 1990, Kirstie Alley played the lead role of Julia Sullivan in the comedy film Madhouse, alongside actors John Larroquette and Melissa Gilbert. The movie was produced by TriStar Pictures and it follows the story of Julia, a woman struggling to cope with the chaos of her personal life. Madhouse was so commercially successful that it held the industry in a chokehold, grossing $45.6 million at the box office.

For Richer or Poorer - 1997 ($63.1 Million)

Via Flickr/Alex's 10-word Movie Review

Kirstie Alley starred as Caroline Tyler, one of the main characters in the 1997 comedy film For Richer or Poorer. The movie, produced by Universal Pictures, tells the story of a wealthy couple who go on the run and pose as Amish to escape the IRS. For Richer or Poorer was a hit at the box office, earning $63.1 million. The film also starred talented actors such as Tim Allen and Jean Smart.

Shoot to Kill - 1988 ($65.3 Million)

In 1988, Kirstie Alley played the role of Sarah Davies, one of the main characters in the action-thriller film Shoot to Kill. The movie, produced by Touchstone Pictures, follows the story of an FBI agent who partners with a skilled tracker to capture a dangerous criminal. Shoot to Kill, which also starred actors Sidney Poitier and Tom Berenger, was successful at the box office as it grossed $65.3 million.

Summer School - 1987 ($83.5 Million)

Via Flickr/TV Insider

Released in 1987, Summer School is a comedy film featuring Kirstie Alley as one of the main characters, Robin Harris. Produced by Paramount Pictures, this movie follows the story of a high school teacher who is reluctantly assigned to teach a summer school class. The appearance of other renowned actors such as Mark Harmon and Carl Reiner also made the movie a hit at the box office, grossing $83.5 million.

Look Who’s Talking Too - 1990 ($103.5 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Look Who's TalkingToo is a comedy film that was released in 1990, featuring Kirstie Alley as the voice of one of the main characters, Mollie. The movie is a sequel to the 1989 film Look Who's Talking, which was produced by TriStar Pictures. It follows the story of a young child as she faces the challenges of growing up. The movie also featured actors John Travolta and Olympia Dukakis and was a commercial success, earning $103.5 million at the box office.

Look Who’s Talking - 1989 ($323.2 Million)

Via Flickr/TigeerStrypes

Look Who's Talking is a comedy film released in 1989 featuring Kirstie Alley as the voice of one of the main characters, Mollie. Produced by TriStar Pictures, this joy-filled movie tells the story of a young child navigating the challenges of growing up. Alongside Kirstie, the movie also featured actors John Travolta and Bruce Willis and was a massive hit, grossing an impressive $323.2 million at the box office.

Star Trek II: The Wrath of Khan - 1982 ($245.9 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Released in 1982, Star Trek II: The Wrath of Khan is a science fiction film in which Kirstie Alley played the role of Saavik, one of the main characters. The movie was produced by Paramount Pictures and it is the second installment in the Star Trek film series. It follows the story of the USS Enterprise crew as they confront the notorious Khan. Other talented acts like William Shatner and Leonard Nimoy also graced the movie with their appearance, making it a commercial success that grossed $245.9 million at the box office.
